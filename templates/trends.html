Machine Learning Model Data

Climate Change data from World Bank database is used to analyze the factors that can influence greenhouse gas emissions. A hypothesis is formulated to test if CO2 emissions depend on country-specific features available in the dataset and can be predicted from these. Machine learning models are built for predicting CO2 emissions from growth indicators like Gross Domestic Product (GDP), population, and fossil fuel usage. Due to the exhibition of nonlinearity of emissions with respect to country-specific features, Decision Tree Regression and Random Forest Regression models are considered. Comparing the root mean square error (RMSE) between the two, Random Forest Regressor(RMSE=0.0493) fits the data better than Decision Tree Regressor (RSME = 0.416). An R-Squared of 99.3% is recorded by Random Forest Regressor.
